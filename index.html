Wu-Tang Clan is a legendary hip-hop group from Staten Island, New York City, formed in 1992. The group is known for their unique style, which blends gritty, street-inspired lyrics with samples from kung-fu movies and soul music.

The group consists of nine core members, each with their own unique style and personality: RZA, GZA, Method Man, Raekwon, Ghostface Killah, Inspectah Deck, U-God, Masta Killa, and Ol' Dirty Bastard (who passed away in 2004). Each member has released solo albums, as well as collaborated on numerous Wu-Tang Clan albums.

The group's debut album, "Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)," is considered a classic in the hip-hop genre, and has been praised for its innovative production and lyricism. The group has also been noted for their influence on the East Coast hip-hop scene and for popularizing the Wu-Tang Clan logo and clothing line.

Wu-Tang Clan remains a popular and influential group in hip-hop today, with their music continuing to inspire new generations of artists.
